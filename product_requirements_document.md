---

### **Project PRD/Brief Template (I will fill this out based on your context)**

---

**1. Document Information**
    *   **Project Title:** [To be filled based on context]
    *   **Date:** [Current Date]
    *   **Version:** 1.0
    *   **Author:** [Your Name/My Name]
    *   **Status:** Draft / Approved / In Progress

**2. Executive Summary**
    *   A high-level overview of the project, problem, solution, and expected impact.

**3. Introduction & Background**
    *   Why is this project being undertaken? What led to its inception?
    *   Context about the current state or market conditions.

**4. Problem Statement**
    *   Clearly articulate the core problem(s) this project aims to solve.
    *   Who experiences this problem? What is the impact if it's not solved?

**5. Goals & Objectives**
    *   **Business Goals:** What strategic business objectives does this project support?
    *   **Product Objectives:** Specific, Measurable, Achievable, Relevant, Time-bound (SMART) objectives for the product/feature.
        *   *Example: Increase user engagement by 20% within 3 months post-launch.*

**6. Target Audience**
    *   Detailed description of the primary and secondary users/customers.
    *   User personas (if applicable).

**7. Solution Overview**
    *   A high-level description of the proposed product or feature.
    *   How does it address the problem statement?
    *   Concept art, wireframes, or flow diagrams (if context allows).

**8. Key Features & Functionality (Detailed Requirements)**
    *   A list of core features, often broken down into functional groups.
    *   For each feature:
        *   **Description:** What does it do?
        *   **User Value:** Why is this feature important to the user?
        *   **Acceptance Criteria:** How will we know it's done correctly? (e.g., "As a [User Role], I want to [Action], so that [Benefit].")

**9. Non-Functional Requirements**
    *   **Performance:** Speed, responsiveness, load handling.
    *   **Security:** Data protection, authentication, authorization.
    *   **Scalability:** Ability to handle future growth.
    *   **Usability:** Ease of use, intuitiveness, accessibility.
    *   **Reliability:** Uptime, error handling, recovery.
    *   **Maintainability:** Ease of updating and fixing.
    *   **Compatibility:** Browser, device, operating system support.

**10. Success Metrics & KPIs**
    *   How will we measure the success of the project against its goals and objectives?
    *   *Examples: Conversion rate, retention rate, task completion time, NPS, revenue impact.*

**11. Scope (In & Out)**
    *   **In-Scope:** What is definitely part of this release/project?
    *   **Out-of-Scope:** What is explicitly NOT part of this release/project (to manage expectations and prevent scope creep)?

**12. Technical Considerations & Dependencies**
    *   Any specific technology requirements, integrations, or platforms.
    *   Dependencies on other teams, external vendors, or existing systems.

**13. Risks & Assumptions**
    *   **Risks:** Potential challenges or roadblocks that could impact the project (technical, market, operational).
    *   **Assumptions:** What are we taking for granted that, if proven false, could impact the project?

**14. Open Questions & Future Considerations**
    *   Any decisions still pending? Areas requiring further research?
    *   Potential next steps or features for future iterations.

**15. Stakeholders & Approvals**
    *   List of key stakeholders and their roles.
    *   Sign-off section for required approvals.
